# World Cup Pool — Rules & Participant Policy

**Document version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** May 2026  
**Organizer:** IoT4B (private internal pool)

This document explains how the World Cup Pool works and the conditions for participating. By logging in and placing predictions, you agree to these rules and the policy below.

---

## 1. Purpose

The World Cup Pool is a **private, invitation-only** prediction game for the FIFA World Cup. It is intended for fun and friendly competition among invited participants only. It is **not** a public gambling service and does not offer cash prizes unless explicitly announced in writing by the organizer.

---

## 2. Eligibility

- Participation is **by invitation only**. You must receive an email invite from the organizer and complete account setup.
- You must use the **email address** that was invited.
- On first login you must set a **personal password** (the temporary password from your email is for one-time use only).
- The organizer may **deactivate** any account at any time, with or without notice.
- Only **one account per person** is allowed.

---

## 3. How to participate

1. Open the link provided by the organizer and log in with your email and password.
2. Go to **My Bets** to view matches and enter predictions.
3. Optionally complete **Bonus** predictions (tournament winner, top scorer, etc.).
4. Check the **Leaderboard** for overall standings.

Predictions are submitted as an **exact score** for each match (home goals and away goals). The system determines winner, draw, or loss from those numbers.

---

## 4. Match predictions

### What you predict

For each match you enter:

- **Home team score** (0–20)
- **Away team score** (0–20)

Examples:

| Your prediction | Meaning |
|-----------------|--------|
| Brazil 2 – Germany 1 | Brazil wins |
| Brazil 1 – Germany 1 | Draw |
| Brazil 0 – Germany 2 | Germany wins |

### Deadlines

- Predictions must be submitted **before kickoff**.
- After kickoff, or if the organizer locks a match, predictions are **closed** for that game.
- You may **change** your prediction until the match is locked or kickoff time passes.

### Scoring (match points)

Points are calculated automatically when the organizer enters the official final score.

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| **Exact score** (both goals correct) | **5** |
| **Correct winner** (right team wins, wrong score) | **3** |
| **Correct draw** (both teams draw, wrong score) | **3** |
| Wrong outcome (wrong winner or wrong draw) | **0** |

**Examples**

- Official: Brazil 2–1 Germany. You predicted 2–1 → **5 points**.
- Official: Brazil 2–1 Germany. You predicted 3–0 → **3 points** (correct winner).
- Official: 1–1. You predicted 2–2 → **3 points** (correct draw).
- Official: 2–1. You predicted 1–2 → **0 points**.

---

## 5. Bonus predictions (tournament)

Each participant may submit **one set** of bonus picks for the active tournament:

| Category | Points (if correct) |
|----------|---------------------|
| World Cup winner (team) | **10** |
| Top scorer (player) | **10** |
| Best goalkeeper (player) | **7** |
| MVP (player) | **7** |

Bonus points are awarded when the organizer confirms official tournament results and runs scoring. Bonus picks should be submitted early; the organizer may close bonus entry before the tournament ends.

---

## 6. Leaderboard and ranking

- Standings show **total points** (match predictions + bonus predictions).
- Rankings are **overall** for the active tournament.
- In case of a tie, the organizer may apply additional tie-break rules (not shown in the app) or leave participants tied.
- The leaderboard is updated after match results are entered and scored.

---

The organizer and designated administrators may:

- Add or change matches, teams, and schedules  
- Lock or unlock predictions  
- Enter final scores and trigger automatic scoring  
- Invite or deactivate users  
- Change point values in system settings (participants will be informed if rules change materially)  

**Administrator decisions are final** for running the pool, interpreting these rules, and resolving disputes.
